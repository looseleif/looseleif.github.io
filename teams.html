CHOICE Robotics Lab

Graduate Researcher

Mar 2022 - Jul 2024

In the CHOICE Robotics Lab, I contributed to research focused on robotic perception and manipulation, particularly high-occlusion mechanical search. My work extended the Grasping the Invisible (GTI) Pipeline and Image-Oriented Search and Grasp (IOSG). Through my project, AGGRO, I developed a scaled robotic platform, advancing exploration and discovery of the target, and facilitating GTI's singulation and grasping processes. This development included the use of open-source robotic hardware and custom implementations for end-effector and visual integration. The research culminated in my Master's Thesis as part of my Master's in Electronics Engineering.

Solar Vehicle Project

Infotainment & Telemetry Engineer, Photography and Video Production

Sep 2021 - Jun 2022

As part of the University of Minnesota Solar Vehicle Project, I contributed to the development of both firmware and hardware solutions for the 14th and 15th vehicle generations, as well as photography and video production throughout the Formula Sun Grand Prix (FSGP) and American Solar Challenge 2022. My work involved user-end implementations for the car's infotainment system, along with designing and populating a four-layer PCB in-house using XBee, Bluetooth, and CAN communication for real-time telemetry. We also integrated SD cards and uninterrupted power supply features using supercapacitors to ensure continued operation in the event of a power loss from the low-voltage bus.
